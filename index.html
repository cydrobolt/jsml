FAQ

Some frequently asked questions by parents and students.

What work will be accomplished at JumpStart Main Line?

Students come in Monday with nothing. We take them through introductions on this type of writing, some examples, and idea-generation exercises, and then they write and conference, conference and write, until Thursday.

Over 80% of our students leave on Thursday with the Common App essay completed. The remaining 20% can email with us until the end of July with questions and revisions. If they finish early (before Thursday), they can start any supplemental essay also, and we will be happy to conference with them about that; about 25% of students do finish the Common App essay early and end up working with us on a second essay.

What if I am applying to a non-Common App college?

JumpStart’s teachers will work with you on a college essay of your choice – the Common App is simply the most common. Non-Common App schools often require a personal statement or offer another prompt that is very similar to one of the Common App prompts. We have had students complete non-Common App essays every year.

What are the hours for the course?

Each session runs Monday through Thursday, 2.5 hours per day for a total of 10 hours for the session.

Does the course have one-on-one or group instruction?

Both. We open with some group instruction for introduction and context and for idea-generation exercises, but even on Day 1 we meet one-on-one with each student. Day 2 is almost entirely one-on-one, and Days 3 and 4 are entirely one-on-one. We plan to conference with each student each day, multiple times with each student per day if possible and if the student requests multiple conferences.

How large are the classes?

The maximum class size is 12 students per session. Both teachers are present, so the student- teacher ratio is 6:1.

How much does the course cost?

The camp costs $500 for the four-day session.

Where does JumpStart Main Line meet?

At Radnor High School, in Radnor, PA. Google Maps

Is JumpStart Main Line only for Radnor students?

No – all are welcome! We have taught students from Radnor HS, Lower Merion HS, Harriton HS, Haverford HS, Methacton HS, Great Valley HS, St. Joseph’s Preparatory School, and several others. If you can get to us, we will work with you!
