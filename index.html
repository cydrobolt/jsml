FAQ

Some questions that students and parents ask frequently.

What work will be accomplished at JumpStart Main Line?

Students come in Monday with nothing. We take you through introductions on this type of writing, some examples, and idea-generation exercises, and then you write and conference, conference and write, until Thursday.

Over 80% of our students leave on Thursday with the Common App essay completed. The remaining 20% can email with us until the end of July with questions and revisions. If you finish early (before Thursday), you can start any supplemental essay also, and we will be happy to conference with you about that; about 25% of students do finish the Common App essay early and end up working with us on a second essay.

What if I am applying to a non-Common App college?

JumpStart’s teachers will work with you on a college essay of your choice – the Common App is simply the most...well, common. Well-named, as it turns out! Non-Common App schools often require a personal statement or offer another prompt that is very similar to one of the Common App prompts. We have had students complete non-Common App essays every year.

Does the course have one-on-one or group instruction?

Both. We open with some group instruction for introduction and context and for idea-generation exercises, but even on Day 1 we meet one-on-one with each student. Day 2 is almost entirely one-on-one, and Days 3 and 4 are entirely one-on-one. We plan to conference with each student each day, multiple times with each student per day if possible and if the student requests multiple conferences.

How large are the classes?

A full class is 12 students. Both teachers are present, so the student-teacher ratio is no greater than 6:1.

How much does the course cost?

The camp costs $515 for the four-day session.

Do I have to bring a laptop computer?

No, but of course you can. Many students do. We even prefer to do some of the early work with good old-fashioned paper and pen – which we provide. Eventually, you’re going to want to word-process, but you can certainly type at home, print up a draft, and bring in the paper copy to conference over and add to.
